# Topic

从低利率到利率再定价：成熟养老金市场的流动性风险、杠杆约束与资产负债管理重构。以 2022 年英国 LDI 危机为核心事件，论证养老金"去风险化"策略如何在降低偿付波动的同时集中并放大了流动性脆弱性。

# Core Question

Why can pension de-risking transform solvency risk into liquidity risk?

# Intended Thesis

De-risking through leveraged LDI strategies reduces measured funding-ratio volatility, but the leverage and collateral mechanics it relies on concentrate liquidity fragility — so that a rapid rate repricing converts a solvency-hedging structure into a source of systemic liquidity stress.

# Paper Type

single_event_mechanism

# Scope

- Period: 2020–2023, centered on September–October 2022
- Region: United Kingdom (gilt market)
- Actors: defined-benefit pension funds, LDI managers, the Bank of England, gilt market intermediaries
- Mechanisms: interest-rate hedging via leveraged LDI, collateral/margin calls, forced gilt sales, fire-sale feedback loops

# Exclusions

- Do not generalize to all mature pension markets without explicit qualifiers.
- Do not give direct China policy prescriptions.
- Do not claim fund-level causal estimation without fund-level data.
- Do not treat this single event as proof of a universal mechanism.

# Seed Claims

1. UK LDI strategies created margin-call liquidity pressure during rapid gilt yield movements.
2. Pension de-risking may reduce funding volatility while increasing liquidity fragility.
3. Solvency/funding risk and liquidity risk are distinct risk categories that LDI links together.
4. Leverage and collateral mechanics connect gilt yield movements to forced liquidity demand.
5. The Bank of England intervention was aimed at market functioning, not pension solvency.
6. Regulatory frameworks measured solvency risk but did not constrain liquidity mismatch in LDI structures.
