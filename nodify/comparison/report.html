The discipline is the biggest single lever — a disciplined agent with no tooling already beats raw by a full point, mostly by forcing an adversarial line and honest calibration. The tree cited twice the sources and kept all 66 KB of them archived and machine-verified — 26 verbatim quotes, every one checked by nd check against its source (100% vs 92% "by diligence"). Raw, by contrast, leaned on three secondary reproductions because publishers blocked the primaries — a provenance gap the archive closes. Notably, the discipline alone already cut context load ~4× vs raw; the tree stays lean too, by pushing raw fetching into isolated subagents.

The honest answer

Yes, the logic tree improves outcomes — but attribute the gains correctly.

  • The thinking discipline is the big lever for prose quality. Most of the jump comes from method, not machinery — it's available with or without the tree.
  • The tree's edge is not smoother writing. It is evidence depth (2× the sources), enforced auditability (100% verbatim, full traceability, no secondary reproductions), and a reasoning state that survives compaction.
  • It puts a floor under rigor that doesn't need a good day. Its checks caught a coverage gap the prose hid; its brief let a cold agent resume in one call. Those compound exactly where discipline alone degrades — at scale, under interruption, with weaker models, or when the output must be trusted and audited, not just read.

Caveat, stated plainly: one clean run per arm, and Opus is a strong researcher — raw still produced a serviceable article (3.0). The tree is not magic. It is insurance against the days the discipline slips.

Method: identical prompt/model/deliverable; only the framework varied. Judges were blind to arm and to the experiment's purpose; articles were mode-hidden with citation formats normalized. Disclosed instrument bug: the first blinding pass renumbered inline citations but not the reference list, introducing a misalignment for the raw/skills arms only; caught on review, corrected to preserve each author's own numbering, and the panel re-run clean — the correction raised raw's evidence score but left the ranking unchanged (tree > skills > raw in both rounds).
