VERBATIM EXTRACTS (task-level exposure with split outcomes):

- "Around the world, 40% of all employment is affected in some way by AI, with the number rising to 60% in higher-income countries."
- "Roughly half the exposed jobs may benefit from AI integration, enhancing productivity. For the other half, AI applications may execute key tasks currently performed by humans, which could lower labor demand, leading to lower wages and reduced hiring."
- Kristalina Georgieva (IMF Managing Director): AI could "jumpstart productivity, boost global growth and raise incomes," yet "it could also replace jobs and deepen inequality."

Additional figures from the IMF SDN (widely reported): emerging market exposure ~40%, low-income country exposure ~26%; "In most scenarios, AI will likely worsen overall inequality."
