SUPPORTS/REFUTES N-0004 (both sources together illustrate the core measurement problem):
- SUPPORTS: Named, dated, employer-confirmed cases exist (Salesforce cutting customer-service headcount ~44% citing AI agents by name; 7,000 AI-cited cuts in a single month, Sept 2025); entry-level tech hiring is explicitly and repeatedly described by Challenger's own SVP as harder due to AI.
- REFUTES/COMPLICATES (core adversarial-honesty finding): explicit AI attribution is a genuinely SMALL and inconsistent share of total layoffs (75 out of ~20,000+ "technology" cuts in H1 2025; ~17,000 of ~600,000+ cuts through September 2025) — but Challenger itself argues this likely UNDERSTATES true AI impact because employers deliberately avoid the "AI" label for PR reasons, meaning the true causal share is unknown and could run either way. This is the central attribution-uncertainty caveat for N-0004.
