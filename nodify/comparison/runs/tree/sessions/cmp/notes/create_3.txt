--- NOTES ---
This source shows concrete, recent (2023-2025) demand-side evidence of new AI-specific roles (AI engineer) growing fast on a major labor-market platform (LinkedIn), consistent with WEF's "AI and Machine Learning Specialists" appearing among fastest-growing occupations (see create_1.txt). However, the same article explicitly flags a contradiction: overall entry-level hiring in the U.S. was falling even as AI-titled roles grew, i.e., the newly created AI roles are a narrow, high-skill slice and do not by themselves establish that creation is offsetting broader labor-market substitution/displacement. This is useful as both supporting evidence (new-role creation is real and fast-growing) and as a limiting caveat (scale of creation vs. scale of displacement is contested).
