--- RELEVANCE ---
Node N-0003 (macro/sector net employment 2020-2025): REFUTES the framing of a large net job-quantity effect from generative AI at the aggregate/sectoral level — ILO's own conclusion is that AUGMENTATION (task-level change within jobs) dominates over full automation/job elimination, and that only clerical work shows meaningfully high exposure; most occupations show low (1-4%) high-exposure task shares. This implies no broad, cross-sector net headcount effect should yet be expected/observed — effects are concentrated and mostly qualitative (job quality/intensity), not quantity.
Node N-0006 (measurement/lag): Supports the claim indirectly — ILO frames its findings as exposure POTENTIAL (a forward-looking task-exposure index), explicitly stating the job QUALITY effects (harder to measure in short windows: work intensity, autonomy) "might be of greater consequence than the quantitative impacts" — i.e., the paper itself does not claim to have measured realized net employment effects, and suggests the larger effects may be qualitative/hard-to-measure rather than clean quantity/headcount shifts separable from other factors.
