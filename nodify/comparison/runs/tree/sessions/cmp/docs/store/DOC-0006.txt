KEY FINDING: A 2024 professional-body member poll of real working freelance translators/interpreters
(CIOL, UK) found over 70% reporting decreased work volume in 2024, with 49% reporting a "significant
decrease"; qualitative testimony describes remaining work shifting from full translation to
lower-value MT post-editing (MTPE) and at least one member reporting exit from the profession —
this is a directly measured, realized demand-side effect in a real market, complementing the
Upwork writing-freelancer decline (real_2.txt) as evidence that translation is an exposed/affected
category, not an unaffected control category.

WHAT'S MISSING: This is a member-poll (self-selected respondents, likely skewed toward those
motivated to respond, i.e. possibly those most affected) rather than platform-transaction data like
Hui/Reshef/Zhou's Upwork study — so it is suggestive/corroborating survey evidence, not as
rigorous/causal as the Upwork natural-experiment design. Sample size and exact survey date were not
stated on the fetched page. The CEPR machine-translation/employment-growth study and the UK Society
of Authors survey look highly relevant but were only found via WebSearch snippets, not independently
fetched/verified — follow up with direct fetches of https://cepr.org/voxeu/columns/lost-translation-ais-impact-translators-and-foreign-language-skills
if stronger quantitative causal evidence on translation specifically is needed.
